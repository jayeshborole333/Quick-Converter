Kilogram

Definition: A kilogram (kg) is the base unit of mass in the International System of Units (SI), equal to 1000 grams.

History/origin: The kilogram was established in 1795 and originally defined as the mass of one liter of water at 4°C.

Current use: The kilogram is the standard unit for mass globally, used in science, commerce, and daily life outside of the U.S.

Pound

Definition: A pound (lb) is a unit of mass in the imperial system, defined as exactly 0.45359237 kilograms.

History/origin: The pound dates back to Roman libra, standardized differently across regions before being fixed internationally in 1959.

Current use: Primarily used in the U.S. and UK to measure body weight, groceries, and packages.
