Liter

Definition: A liter (symbol: L) is a unit of volume accepted for use with the International System of Units (SI), but not a SI unit itself. It is equal to 1 cubic decimeter (1 dm³), or 1,000 cubic centimeters (cm³).

History/origin: The liter was introduced in France in 1795 as part of the metric system and originally defined as one cubic decimeter. It was redefined in 1964 based on the cubic decimeter.

Current use: The liter is used worldwide to measure liquid volumes, especially in food and beverage packaging, fuel consumption, and scientific research.

Gallon (US)

Definition: A US gallon (symbol: gal) is a unit of volume in the US customary system. It is defined as exactly 231 cubic inches or 3.785411784 liters.

History/origin: The US gallon originates from the English wine gallon used in the 1700s. The British and American gallons differ in size; the US gallon is smaller.

Current use: The US gallon is used mainly in the United States to measure volumes of fuel, beverages, and containers, especially in retail and transportation.
