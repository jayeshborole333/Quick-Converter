Inch

Definition: An inch (symbol: in) is a unit of length in the imperial and US customary systems, equal to 1/12 of a foot or 2.54 centimeters.

History/origin: Derived from the width of a human thumb. Standardized in the 1950s to exactly 2.54 cm.

Current use: Common in the US and UK for small measurements like screen sizes, paper sizes, etc.

Yard

Definition: A yard (symbol: yd) is 36 inches or 0.9144 meters.

Current use: Used in field-length measurements in sports and in daily use in the US and UK.
