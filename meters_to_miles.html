Mile

Definition: A mile (symbol: mi) is a unit of length in the imperial and US customary systems, equal to 1,609.344 meters or 1.609 kilometers.

History/origin: Derived from the Roman "mille passus" (a thousand paces), evolving through various regional definitions until standardized in 1959.

Current use: Still widely used in the US and UK for distances in road transport and sports (e.g., mile runs).
