Pound

Definition: A pound (symbol: lb) is a unit of mass used in the imperial and US customary systems. One pound is defined as exactly 16 ounces or 0.45359237 kilograms.

History/origin: The pound originated from the Roman libra. Various definitions existed in Europe, but the avoirdupois system (used today) was defined in 1959 by international agreement.

Current use: The pound is widely used in the United States for body weight, packaged foods, and industrial products. It is also used in the UK alongside the metric system.

Ounce

Definition: An ounce (symbol: oz) is 1/16 of a pound or exactly 28.349523125 grams in the avoirdupois system.

History/origin: The ounce stems from the Roman uncia, used as one-twelfth of a Roman pound. It evolved into different systems in Europe, with the modern avoirdupois ounce adopted in 1959.

Current use: Ounces are used in the US and UK to measure packaged food, postal items, and more. It is often used for smaller weight measurements.
