Gram

Definition: A gram (g) is a unit of mass in the SI system, defined as one-thousandth of a kilogram. It's based on Planck’s constant since 2019.

History/origin: Defined as the mass of 1 cm³ of water at 4 °C. It became part of SI in 1960, later redefined based on Planck’s constant.

Current use: Used globally for weighing food, groceries, medicine, and science. Appears on nutrition labels to indicate per-100g values.

Ounce

Definition: An ounce (oz) is a unit of mass in the imperial and US customary systems. One ounce is equal to 28.34952 grams.

History/origin: Derived from the Roman “uncia” (1/12 of a Roman pound). The current avoirdupois ounce was adopted in 1959.

Current use: Common in the US for food labeling, groceries, and postal weights. Also used in some UK contexts like restaurant menus.
