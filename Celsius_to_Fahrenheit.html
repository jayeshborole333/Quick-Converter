Celsius

Definition: Celsius (°C) is a metric temperature scale where 0°C is water's freezing point and 100°C is boiling point at 1 atm pressure.

History/origin: Introduced in 1742 by Anders Celsius, this scale was originally reversed. It was later inverted to the current format.

Current use: Widely used globally for daily temperature measurement, weather, cooking, and science, except in a few countries like the US.

Fahrenheit

Definition: Fahrenheit (°F) is a temperature scale where water freezes at 32°F and boils at 212°F under standard atmospheric pressure.

History/origin: Invented by Daniel Fahrenheit in 1724, it used a mercury thermometer based on ice-salt and human body temperature.

Current use: Primarily used in the US for weather forecasts, ovens, and thermostats; less common elsewhere.

How to Convert Celsius to Fahrenheit

F = (9/5) × C + 32

C = (5/9) × (F − 32)

Example: convert 15 °C to °F:
15 × 9/5 + 32 = 59 °F
