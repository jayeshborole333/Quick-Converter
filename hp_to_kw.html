Horsepower

Definition: Horsepower (hp) is a unit of power. 1 mechanical hp = 745.7 watts; 1 metric hp = 735.5 watts.

History/origin: Invented by James Watt in the 18th century to compare steam engine output to draft horses.

Current use: Still widely used in automotive industries to describe engine output.

Kilowatt

Definition: A kilowatt (kW) is a unit of power equal to 1,000 watts or 1.341 hp.

History/origin: From the metric system, used globally for measuring electrical and mechanical power.

Current use: Commonly used for appliances, motors, and electrical systems worldwide.
