Radian

Definition: A radian is the angle made by taking the radius and wrapping it along the edge of a circle. 1 radian ≈ 57.2958°.

History/origin: Introduced in the 18th century, the radian provides a natural way to measure angles based on arc length and radius.

Current use: Radians are the standard unit of angular measure in mathematics, physics, and engineering.

Degree

Definition: A degree is 1/360 of a full circle. 1 radian = 180/π degrees.

History/origin: Originated with the Babylonians who used base-60 (sexagesimal) numbering. The division into 360 parts was influenced by the approximate number of days in a year.

Current use: Degrees are commonly used in geometry, navigation, and everyday angle measurements.

How to Convert Radians to Degrees
