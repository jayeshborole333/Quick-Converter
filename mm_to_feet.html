Millimeter

Definition: A millimeter (mm) is one-thousandth of a meter (0.001 m).

History/origin: Part of the metric system established in France in the 18th century.

Current use: Used globally for measuring small lengths like mechanical parts, tools, and technical dimensions.

Foot

Definition: A foot (symbol: ft) is a unit of length in the imperial and US customary systems. 1 foot = 0.3048 meters.

History/origin: Based on human foot length. Standardized in 1959.

Current use: Widely used in the US for height, room dimensions, and more. Also used in aviation and some construction globally.
