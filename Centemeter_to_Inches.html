Centimeter

Definition: A centimeter (symbol: cm) is a unit of length in the International System of Units (SI), the current form of the metric system. It is defined as 1/100 meters.

History/origin: A centimeter is based on the SI unit meter, and as the prefix "centi" indicates, is equal to one hundredth of a meter.

Current use: The centimeter is widely used worldwide in countries that follow the metric system, particularly to measure height, small objects, or distances.

Inch

Definition: An inch (symbol: in) is a unit of length in the imperial and US customary systems of measurement. An inch is exactly equal to 25.4 millimeters.

History/origin: The word "inch" comes from the Latin "uncia" meaning one-twelfth of a Roman foot.

Current use: Inches are mostly used in the US, UK, and Canada, especially for measuring height, screen sizes, and in construction industries.
