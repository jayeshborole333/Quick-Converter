Kilometer

Definition: A kilometer (symbol: km) is a unit of length in the metric system equal to 1,000 meters.

History/origin: Introduced as part of the metric system in France in the late 18th century. The "kilo-" prefix denotes a factor of 1,000.

Current use: Used around the world for measuring long distances, especially in mapping and road transportation.

Meter

Definition: The meter is the base unit of length in SI, defined by the distance light travels in 1/299,792,458 of a second.

Current use: Standard unit for most distance-related measurements globally.
