Yard

Definition: A yard (symbol: yd) is a unit of length equal to 3 feet or 36 inches. Defined as 0.9144 meters since 1959.

History/origin: English in origin, based on the distance from the tip of the nose to the outstretched thumb. Standardized over time.

Current use: Widely used in the US and UK, especially in sports and land measurement.

Mile

Definition: A mile (symbol: mi) is a unit of length equal to 5,280 feet or 1,760 yards.

History/origin: Derived from the Roman "mille passus" meaning "thousand paces".

Current use: Used for measuring distances in the UK and US, mainly in road travel.
