Miles per Hour

Definition: Miles per hour (symbol: mph) is a unit of speed expressing the number of miles traveled in one hour.

History/origin: The unit comes from the mile, originally defined in Roman times and later standardized as 5,280 feet. “Per hour” came into use as clocks became more common for measuring time.

Current use: mph is commonly used in the United States, the United Kingdom, and some other countries to express vehicle speeds and speed limits.

Kilometers per Hour

Definition: Kilometers per hour (symbol: km/h or kph) is a metric unit of speed expressing the number of kilometers traveled in one hour.

History/origin: The unit is derived directly from the metric system, with the kilometer and hour standardized in the 19th century.

Current use: km/h is the standard unit of speed in most countries worldwide, used in road signage and transportation systems.

How to Convert MPH to KPH
