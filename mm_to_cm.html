Millimeter

Definition: A millimeter (mm) is a unit of length in the metric system, equal to one-thousandth of a meter.

History/origin: Introduced during the French Revolution when the metric system was established. Based on decimal multiples of the meter.

Current use: Commonly used for small measurements like component sizes, technical drawings, and precision tools worldwide.

Centimeter

Definition: A centimeter (cm) is equal to one-hundredth of a meter.

History/origin: Part of the metric system since its inception. “Centi” stands for one-hundredth.

Current use: Used globally to measure small lengths such as height, width, and distance in everyday use and science.

How to Convert Millimeter to Centimeter

1 mm = 0.1 cm

1 cm = 10 mm

Example: convert 120 mm to cm:

120 mm = 120 × 0.1 = 12 cm
