Millimeter

Definition: A millimeter (symbol: mm) is a unit of length in the International System of Units (SI). It is defined as one-thousandth of a meter.

History/origin: The "milli-" prefix means 1/1000. The millimeter has been used since the adoption of the metric system. Earlier, the meter was based on a physical prototype; today it is defined by the distance light travels in a vacuum in 1/299,792,458 of a second.

Current use: Millimeters are widely used for small-scale measurements in mechanical engineering, machining, and design, especially where fine precision is required.

Meter

Definition: The meter (symbol: m) is the SI base unit of length. It is currently defined as the distance traveled by light in a vacuum in 1/299,792,458 seconds.

History/origin: Adopted in 1793 and redefined over time to improve precision, including the 2019 redefinition based on the speed of light.

Current use: Used globally in science, industry, and daily life, except in the United States where US customary units dominate.
