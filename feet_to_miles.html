Foot

Definition: A foot (symbol: ft) is a unit of length in the imperial and US customary systems. One foot equals 12 inches or 0.3048 meters.

History/origin: Derived from the average human foot size, it was standardized in 1959 to exactly 0.3048 meters.

Current use: Commonly used in the US and UK for height, elevation, and shorter distances.

Mile

Definition: A mile (mi) is 5280 feet or exactly 1609.344 meters.

History/origin: Derived from the Roman “mille passus,” meaning “a thousand paces.”

Current use: Primarily used in the US and UK for road distances and travel.
